n is the number of periods. In this case it varies depending on the calculation for daily monthly, yearly and five years.

Find Arithmetic and Geometric mean of S&P500

  • Arithmetic Mean formula is: (Return1 + Return2 + ... + Returnn)/n Arithmetic Mean is calculated by summing the daily returns for every month or year and divided by corresponding number of periods.
  • As we are using daily return for S&P500, daily return needs to be compounded to get the monthly return, which then can be used to calculate arithmetic mean. Same approach for arithmetic mean of yearly and 5 years.
  • The geometric formula is: [(1+Return1) x (1+Return2) x (1+Return3)...)]1/n - 1. By compounding daily return, monthly return is obtained and hence can be used to calcuate monthly geometric mean. Same approach can be used to calculate yearly return and five years return.

Find Arithmetic and Geometric mean of Excess Return

  • Download monthly return for 30 days, 1 year and 5 years from CRSP. Compound the return for 1 year and 5 years to get a series of 1 year and 5 years frequency. Minus the return from the calculated S&P500 return for 30 days, 1 year and 5 years accordingly. Annualize the return to arithmetic and geometric mean.
  • Download daily return for Federal Fund rate, and minus the data from S&P500 to get dailly excess return. Annualized the data to geometric and arithmetic mean.
