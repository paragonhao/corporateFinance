Executive Summary

The purpose of the assignment is to find whether there is an impact on the return of the stock before and after the ex-dividend date. I have gathered all the S&P 500 companies that have given dividends from 2011/01/01 to 2017/12/31 to understand the arithmetic mean return from -20 day to +20 day taking ex-divdate as day 0.

Implementation And Methdology

Detailed Implementation in R is available on my github here

Assumptions: Use Return on stock without dividend so that the fall in return after ex-div date can be clearly observed.
  • Step 1: Download data from CRSP, data is available from my Dropbox
  • Step 2: Identify the ex-div dates: ex-div dates are the dates in the rows where column Payment Date is not NA.
  • Step 3: Slice the data into vectors of 41 days. Add all the vectors together to get the arithmetic mean of the 41 days. Subtract the daily return of S&P500 to get the excess return.
  • NOTE: There is a small chunk of data that are NAs from the excess return calculation. I assume 0s for the NA data. This would be a fair assumption since the dataset is large, hence replacing NAs with 0 would not impact the mean significantly.
